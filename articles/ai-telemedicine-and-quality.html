AI and Telemedicine

AI, telemedicine, and maintaining quality in care

Published: 18 January 2025 6 to 8 minutes

AI will shape remote care; quality depends on clear triage, escalation, auditability, and human judgement at the right points.

AITelemedicineClinical governanceRisk

Telemedicine is not new; what is changing is the support layer. AI is now being used to summarise notes, suggest pathways, and triage risk. The opportunity is huge. The risk is also real.

Quality in AI supported care depends on boundaries. What can be safely automated; what must remain clinician led; what needs a second check. In governance we often describe this as designing safe points of control; not to restrict practice, but to protect patients.

Remote care needs clear triage and escalation rules. You need to know what cannot be managed remotely; what red flags trigger urgent review; and where responsibility sits. If an AI tool suggests a pathway; the clinician should still understand the reasoning, and the documentation should show what was decided and why.

Auditability matters. If a decision is challenged later, you need an evidence trail; who assessed; what information was used; what was advised; what follow up was arranged. This is where digital documentation and quality improvement meet.

Public health thinking helps here because it encourages responsible adoption; we ask, who benefits, who might be excluded, and what safeguards reduce unintended harm. AI can support safer care, but only when it is governed well.
